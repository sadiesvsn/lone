About

Bobby Wardel Sandimanie III aka Destroy Lonely is the GOAT of the new gen. His father is Bobby Sandimanie, who is better known as I-20. Lonely signed to Playboi Carti's label Opium in 2021. He is often featured with Ken Carson. He is well known for his song No Stylist which made it into the Billboard 200. In the words of Rolling Stone magazine, he is recognized by his "dynamic yet blaring" instrumentals and his unique aesthetic of dark clothing and high-end clothing .

Early Life

On July 30, 2001, Bobby Wardel Sandimanie III was born in Atlanta, Georgia. He mentions having two younger sisters and an elder brother, but only as half-siblings. He talks of feeling more like an orphan. He was inspired to freestyle by his father, American rapper I-20, as he was growing up. His grandmother, a former teacher, gave him permission to start homeschooling in the sixth grade. In the ninth and tenth years when he returned to school, he remembers misusing the drug Xanax. The "Destroy" component of his stage name is said to have sprung from his belief that his substance abuse was destroying him. He spent most of his early years in solitude which is the origin of "Lonely."

The Beginning 2015-2019

Lonely at the age of 14, began writing songs and recording them at a school recording studio. At school, he made two of his closest friends, Texaco and Nezzus, who helped him create many of his early works and continued to collaborate with him on later endeavors. They started recording their debut album, NezzusDestroyed, as soon as they met Nezzus. Later on, Lonely's 2019 song "Bane" would become popular. When "Bane" was made and Lonely went live on Instagram he only had 4 viewers. He was so persistent that it is one of his popular songs today. Being only 21, Lonely is gaining so much popularity and the new gen are catching on.
